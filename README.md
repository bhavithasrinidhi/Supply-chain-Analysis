# Supply Chain Analysis — Beauty & Personal Care 💄

## Project Overview
Exploratory Data Analysis on a Beauty & Personal Care supply chain dataset 
to identify revenue drivers, quality issues, inventory risks, and logistics 
efficiency opportunities.

## Dataset
- **Categories:** Skincare, Haircare, Cosmetics
- **Total SKUs:** 101
- **Features:** Price, Revenue, Stock Levels, Defect Rates, Shipping, Transportation

## Tools Used
- **Python** — Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn
- **Google Colab** — Development environment

## Key Findings

| # | Finding | Value |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Top Revenue Category | Skincare — $241,628 (41.6% of total) |
| 2 | Highest Defect Rate | Haircare — 2.48% (29% above lowest) |
| 3 | Inventory Risk | Skincare has only 7.76% stock buffer vs sales |
| 4 | Best Shipping Carrier | Carrier B — cheapest AND fastest |
| 5 | Cheapest Transport Mode | Sea — 34% cheaper than Air |

## Visualizations
- 📊 Bar Chart — Revenue by Product Type
- 📊 Bar Chart — Defect Rate by Product Type

- 📊 Transportation Mode Cost Comparison

## Recommendations
- Increase safety stock for Skincare to avoid stockouts (highest revenue, lowest buffer)
- Investigate quality control process for Haircare production line
- Shift more shipment volume to Carrier B for cost and speed efficiency
- Use Sea transport for non-urgent shipments to cut logistics costs by 34%
- Cosmetics has best quality but lowest revenue — explore marketing opportunities

## Conclusion
Skincare drives the business but carries the highest supply chain risk due to 
thin inventory buffers. Combined with carrier and transport mode optimisation, 
the business can reduce costs while protecting its top revenue category from 
stockouts.
